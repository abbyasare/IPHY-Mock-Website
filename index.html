Welcome to IPHY 3410 | Introduction to Human Anatomy

Dr. Leif Saul

Class Overview

Explores the cells, tissues, and organs that compose the different anatomical systems including integumentary, skeletal, muscular, digestive, respiratory, cardiovascular, lymphatic, nervous, urinary and reproductive. Department enforced prerequisite: one year of general biology (lecture + lab).

Learning Goals

  • Demonstrate understanding of structure/function relationships.
  • Integrate multiple systems to explain how an organ works
  • Predict the epithelial tissue type present in a structure based on function.
  • Connect different concepts presented at different times on the same structure
  • Understand and apply the hierarchical organization of structure
  • Work with images or tactile representations of anatomy
